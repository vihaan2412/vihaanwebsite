How are Rare Earths Extracted

Primary processes

Mining of rare earth minerals is done by the open-pit method. In this method, a vast area is dug until the rare earth mineral containing rocks surface. Then, these rocks are blasted, loaded onto trucks using shovels, and taken to a concentration plant.

In detail...

  1. Exploration: The first step in the open pit mining process is exploration to identify the location and quality of the rare earth ore deposit.

  2. Site preparation: Once a deposit is identified, the site is prepared for mining. This typically involves clearing the area and constructing access roads.

  3. Extraction: The rare earth ore is then extracted from the ground using heavy machinery, such as bulldozers, excavators, and trucks.

  4. Crushing and grinding: The extracted ore is then crushed and ground into a fine powder.

  5. Leaching: The powdered ore is mixed with a chemical solution that dissolves the rare earth elements, leaving behind the other materials.

  6. Purification: The solution is then purified to remove impurities and other unwanted elements.

  7. Precipitation: The rare earth elements are then precipitated out of the purified solution using chemical reagents.

  8. Drying and calcination: The precipitate is dried and calcined, or heated to high temperatures, to remove any remaining water and other impurities.

  9. Site preparation: Once a deposit is identified, the site is prepared for mining. This typically involves clearing the area and constructing access roads.

  10. Refining: The final step is to refine the rare earth elements to the desired purity and composition.

The first step in the open-pit mining process is to identify the mineral deposit and assess its potential. This involves a geological survey to determine the size and location of the deposit and the concentration of REEs. Once the deposit has been identified, the next step is to clear the land and prepare the site for mining. This may involve removing trees and other vegetation, as well as building access roads and facilities for the workers.

The actual mining process begins with drilling and blasting to break up the rock and create a pit. The rock and soil are then removed using large trucks and excavators, and transported to a processing plant where the REEs are extracted. The rock is crushed and ground into a fine powder, and then treated with a chemical solution to separate the REEs from the other minerals.

One of the challenges of extracting REEs by open-pit mining is that the concentration of the elements is often low, which means that a large amount of rock and soil must be mined to extract a small amount of REEs. This can result in significant environmental impacts, including soil erosion, water pollution, and habitat destruction. In addition, the chemical process used to extract the REEs can generate large amounts of waste, which must be carefully managed to prevent contamination of the surrounding environment.

Overall, open-pit mining is a complex and resource-intensive process for extracting rare earth elements. While it has enabled the production of many important high-tech products, it also poses significant environmental challenges and requires careful management to minimize its impact on the surrounding ecosystem.
